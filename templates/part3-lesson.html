Lessons 13-15

So now you're getting familiar with the letters. They ain't that difficult right? Lesson 13, page 19: The next letter is called THaal. It looks like a d but with a dot above it. You put the tip of your tongue between your front teeth. Pronounced similar to the English THE. If you remember on page 11 we took another letter Thaa, ث, which also needs the top of the tongue to stick in between the front teeth.

Lesson 14, page 20: This letter could be confused with the previous letter so pay extra attention to the sound. If you pronounce mother in the American way, the THA sound in mothaa is a heavy sound of thaal (the previous letter page 19). Just a different tone, a magnified sound of the English The.

Lesson 15, page 21: The next letter is a heavy D! It's a unique letter that's why they call Arabic the language of Daad. It is like the sound of the D in DUck but with stress on DU. Thus it is a magnified D.

Lesson 18, page 24: This is a k sound. I like the pictograph I made of this letter. Note that when it comes at the end of Word it has a glottal stop sound. Like the sound at the very end of Book'. That's why it's written with a glottal stop. So not the different shape at the end of a word. Note. Note the different shape.
