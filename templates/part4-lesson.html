Lesson 19-21

Lesson 19, page 25: This letter is an English L sound. Easy to remember as it resembles a leg. Note the full shape when it comes at the end of the word or when standalone has a ring at the bottom.

Now we have taken the 5 letters that are connected only to the preceding letter. ا. و. ر. د. ذ. These letters never connect after. Usually when you write you leave the letter open (open dash, not closed) until you reach the next letter. However with those 5 letters you may close them since they don't get connected to the letter coming after them in a word. Example: دار =Home. The D is closed, the alef also stops and is not connected to the R after it. If we write "gap" in Arabic letters. جاب. The alif gets connected only to the preceding G. Usually you will keep extending the G with an open dash (without closing it with the ring) until you meet the next letter which is an alif in this case. Then since the alif is one of the 5 letters (you can remember them with the word ZADOR) therefore it stops there, then you start with the next letter.

Lessons 22-23

Lesson 22, page 28: straightforward h, same as English h. Notice the difference in shape among the start, middle, and standalone variants. Notice that when it comes at the end of the word (connected to preceding letter) it might be confused with the shape of a special character called the knotted T, ة. That knotted T comes only at the end of a word denoting the female gender of the word (yes words in Arabic have gender!). The confusing shape at the end, resembles an H but with an added two dots (like the letter T. However they both are pronounced H!

Lesson 23, page 28: Same as we did with the twin letters ح and خ. This is another twin. The letter 'A called 'Aayn (which means eye in Arabic). And the twin which comes with the same shape but with a dot above it denoting the negative traits of it! The twin's name is GHayn. The gh has a special pronunciation same like the french pronunciation of the R, a gargling sound that comes from the back ceiling of the mouth. But the good brother 'Ayn needs you to use a muscle in your throat. It can be done if you press gently on your Adam's apple while saying apple. It needs some practice, but it's important at least for the simple fact that the very word Araby or Arabic starts with it!

Words starting with the good brother 'Ayn: Bride, work, car, flag, honey, perfume, mind, justice. Words starting with the naughty brother Ghayn: Strange cheater meager angry stupid heedless mistake. Even the sound of it is not very clean! However, we can still appreciate, I just use this story to fix it in your mind with associations.
